Executive Summary

The U.S. housing market continues to show mixed signals in early 2026. While inventory levels have increased significantly (+9.2% year-over-year), median home prices are experiencing a modest decline (-2.0% YoY) for the first time in several years. The mortgage environment remains dynamic with the Trump administration's proposed 50-year mortgage terms under consideration, potentially reshaping affordability calculations. Delinquency rates remain historically low, indicating continued borrower stability despite economic uncertainties.

Mortgage Environment

50-Year Mortgage Proposal

The Trump administration is actively considering 50-year mortgage terms to improve affordability. FHFA Director Bill Pulte has acknowledged ongoing efforts, though implementation faces regulatory hurdles.

Potential Impact: Lower monthly payments but significantly higher total interest costs over the loan term.

Conforming Loan Limits 2026

FHFA announced updated conforming loan limit values for 2026, allowing borrowers in many areas to qualify for larger conforming mortgages without shifting to jumbo loan financing.

Regulatory Streamlining

New regulatory changes focus on recalibrating AML and Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) compliance requirements, with emphasis on reducing administrative burden while maintaining consumer protections.

Notable Regional/National Shifts

Market Rebalancing

The housing market is experiencing a significant rebalancing after years of extreme seller advantage. Key indicators include:

  • First year-over-year price decline since 2019
  • Inventory levels returning to more normal ranges
  • Reduced bidding wars and competitive offers
  • Longer days on market providing buyers more time to decide

Policy Environment

The new administration's housing policies are taking shape with focus on:

  • Innovative mortgage products (50-year terms)
  • Potential use of retirement funds for down payments
  • Regulatory streamlining to reduce costs
  • Emphasis on increasing homeownership rates
